Foreword

This edition of Hans Christian Andersen’s Fairy Tales was produced from several translations.

“A Picture Book Without Pictures” was translated by Mary Howitt and published in A Picture-Book Without Pictures, and Other Stories, in 1848.

“The Ice-Maiden,” “The Psyche” and “The Snail and the Rosebush” were translated by Fanny Fuller and published in The Ice-Maiden, and Other Tales, in 1861.

“The Wicked Prince” and “The Snowdrop” were translated by Robert Nisbet Bain and published in The Little Mermaid, and Other Tales, in 1893. The introduction for the present edition also comes from this publication.

“Silly Hans,” “‘The Will-o’-the-Wisps Are in Town,’ Said the Woman from the Marsh” and “Aunty Toothache” were translated by Hans Lien Brækstad in 1900.

“The Goloshes of Fortune,” “The Marsh King’s Daughter,” “The Dryad,” “The Thistle’s Experiences,” “Good Luck Can Lie in a Pin,” “The Most Incredible Thing” and “The Great Sea Serpent” were translated by J. K. Craigie and W. A. Craigie and published in Fairy Tales and Other Stories, in 1914.

“The Greenies” and “Sunshine Stories” were translated by J. H. Stickney and published in Hans Andersen’s Fairy Tales, First Series, in 1914. “The Real Princess,” “The Drop of Water,” “The Teapot” and “What Happened to the Thistle” were also translated by Stickney and published in Hans Andersen’s Fairy Tales, Second Series, in 1915.

The remaining stories were translated by H. B. Paull and published in Hans Andersen’s Fairy Tales, in 1888.
